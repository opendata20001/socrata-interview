Veteran Affairs

ASPIRE Ratings

History

ASPIRE is an open-data initiative by the Department of Veteran's Affairs started in 2010. It provides the results of a regular audit of various goals that the VA has for each of it's medical providers across the United States.

To help normalize the data, each goal scored against a percentage from how away it is from reaching their target goal.

Score Relationship to Goal
5 Meets or exceeds goal
4 Less than 10% from goal
3 11%-20% from goal
2 More than 20% difference
1 More than 30% difference

By The Numbers

169 Locations

23 Veteran Integrated Service Networks (VISN)

Each location is part of a VISN.

84 distinct measurements

Low Performing Measurements

What makes this dataset valuable is that it provides a way to help discover which VISN's need the most attention to improve the quality of their care, as well as finding any cross-cutting issues within the system.

For example, the following chart shows the number of low-performing measurements for each VISN. Overall, the rates aren't bad. Most of the VISN's only have one or two measurements that stay low throughout the 5 years that the datasets cover.

However, there are a couple of outliers in the data. VISN 22 (The Desert Pacific Healthcare Network) has it's worst year in 2012 with 4 low-performing measurements. However, it manages to drop down to just two in the most recent sample, which makes it the most improved VISN.

List of VISN-Wide Measurements More Than 20 Percent From Their Goal

In this table, you can sort and explore the specific measurements that are low-performing for each VISN. There are two measurements in particular that stand out in this table as widespread issues across all of the medical facilities in the VA.

  • Efficiency - All cause 30-day unadjusted readmission rate: Consistently an issue across multiple VISN's. 8 out of 23 VISN's in 2014
  • Safety - Healthcare associated infections - VAP infection rate: 6 out of 23 VISN's in 2014, curiously all in the Northeast.
